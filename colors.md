# Colors

Source: primary tones extracted from the Art of Breath logo variants (`Art of Breath Logo Variants 2025/`); secondary tones inherited from the Daniel Alan branding bible (parent brand).

Four warm neutral tones form the Art of Breath core, extended by a set of complementary tones for richer storytelling. The palette is built to evoke groundedness, subtlety, and emotional resonance. The goal: let viewers feel before they think.

## Primary palette

The Art of Breath brand neutrals. Each is the fill color of a named logo variant.

| Name | Hex | Logo variant | Meaning |
|------|-----|--------------|---------|
| Rooted Earth | `#AA9983` | `rooted-earth` | Warm clay-sand grounding tone. Embodiment, safety, the strength of being anchored in the present. |
| Ash Fog | `#7C705C` | `ash-fog` | Muted grey-brown, like smoke settling. Stillness, transition, the quiet of the inner journey. |
| Weathered Bone | `#C1B9A7` | `weathere-bone` | Warm off-white with subtle texture. Clarity, silence, the space between breaths. Presence. |
| Charcoal Smoke | `#383530` | `charcoal-smoke` | Deep charcoal-brown with gravitas. Grounded leadership and the quiet weight of responsibility. A powerful alternative to black. |

A pure **White** logo variant (`white`) also exists for use on dark backgrounds.

## Color scales

Ten-step scales for every palette color. **Step 50 is the canonical brand hex.** Steps 00–40 are tints (toward a warm off-white), 60–100 are shades (toward a warm near-black). Use lighter steps for backgrounds and surfaces, darker steps for text and depth. Stay close to 50 to keep the palette quiet.

Procedure (so it can be reapplied to any future color): blend the base toward the warm off-white anchor `#F7F4F1` for tints and the warm near-black anchor `#27221E` for shades, at these per-step fractions — 00 `.93`, 10 `.75`, 20 `.52`, 30 `.31`, 40 `.14`, 60 `.195`, 70 `.39`, 80 `.585`, 90 `.78`, 100 `.975`.

## Text roles

Semantic roles for type, mapped to the palette. Reference these role names from `typo.md` so type and color stay decoupled but linked.

| Role | Color | Use |
|------|-------|-----|
| `text-primary` | Charcoal Smoke `#383530` | Headings and body on light backgrounds. |
| `text-muted` | Ash Fog `#7C705C` | Captions, supporting and secondary copy. |
| `text-inverse` | Weathered Bone `#C1B9A7` | Text on dark backgrounds. |
| `text-accent` | Ember `#B45C43` | Emphasis, links, emotive highlights. |
| `text-faint` | Ash Fog 40 `#8D8271` | Placeholders, disabled. |

## Usage notes

- Lead with the primary neutrals. Reach for secondary tones to set a theme or add accent.
- Charcoal Smoke replaces pure black.
- Keep contrast soft. The palette is meant to be quiet, not loud.
